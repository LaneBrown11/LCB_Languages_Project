LISP

Basic Information

Created by John McCarthy at Massachusetts Institute of Technology in 1959

Considered the oldest functional language, it was first utilized on an IBM 704 computer. It is
considered very effective for use in artificial intelligence programs due to its ability to process
symbolic information. Lisp has gone through a large number of dialects through the decades, but
in the 1980s, Common Lisp was created as a way to unify the different dialects to streamline the
use of the language. Today, Lisp is still valuable, particularly with AI programming, which is becoming
increasingly more valuable. Lisp is also used for natural language processing, the ability for computers to
interpret human language
